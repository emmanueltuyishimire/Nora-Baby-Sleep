Baby Sleep FAQ

Expert answers to common baby sleep questions

What is the 3-3-3 rule for baby sleep?
The 3-3-3 rule suggests that by 3 months old, babies should be taking 3 naps per day, with 3 hours of awake time between naps, and sleeping for 3-hour stretches at night.
What do you need for baby sleep?
Essential items include a safe crib with firm mattress, fitted sheet, sleep sack or swaddle, white noise machine, blackout curtains, and a consistent bedtime routine.
What is the best baby sleep aid?
White noise machines, sleep sacks, and consistent bedtime routines are among the most effective and safe sleep aids. Avoid sleep positioners or loose bedding.
How to stop baby from waking every hour?
Establish consistent sleep routines, ensure proper feeding during the day, create a sleep-conducive environment, and gradually teach self-soothing skills appropriate for your baby's age.
What is the fastest sleep training method?
The 'Cry It Out' (Extinction) method can show results in 3-7 days, but gentler methods like 'Ferber' or 'Chair Method' may take 1-2 weeks with less stress for both baby and parents.
